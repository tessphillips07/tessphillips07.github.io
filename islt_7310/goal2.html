Goal 2

Cultivate an LGBTQ+ and gender-inclusive learning environment.

Creating an inclusive environment means being in the know about terminology students prefer to go by.

Practice using new language when referencing the whole class, such as “students”, “scholars” or “friends”. Avoid using phrases such as “boys and girls” or “guys” when referring to the group.

Shift your language to include family discussions as well. When referring to a students' caretaker, avoid using “mom”, “dad” or “parents”.

Students come from many backgrounds that may not have that lifestyle at home. Instead, change the language for “adult”, “guardian” or “caretaker”. This adheres to all family structures and gender identities.

Group students together for activities or games using methods such as birthdays, favorite colors, or current color of clothing rather than gender.
